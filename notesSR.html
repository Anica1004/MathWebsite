Square Roots

1. Square Roots

    1.1 What is a Square Root?

Have you ever thought about figuring out a question like this: "What number do you need to multiply by itself to get 25?"

Solving that might seem tricky, which is why we introduce square roots to make it easier!

A Square Root is a mathematical operation that helps us find a number which, when multiplied by itself, gives us another number.
More precisely, the Square Root of a number is another number that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number.

For Example:

The square root of 9 is 3 because 3 × 3 = 9
The square root of 16 is 4 because 4 × 4 = 16.
The square root of 25 is 5 because 5 × 5 = 25.

The Square Root Symbol is used to represent the square root of any number.

Square Root Symbol: \(\sqrt{x}\)
where x is called the radicand

We can simply write the square root of 25 as \(\sqrt{25}\)

Again, if we rewrite our previous example with the square root symbol..

\(\sqrt{9} = 3\)
\(\sqrt{16} = 4\)
\(\sqrt{25} = 5\)

As shown in the example, some numbers have whole number square roots, like 4 (whose square root is 2) and 9 (whose square root is 3). These are called perfect squares.

For these perfect squares, simply consider the root as undoing the square process.

\(\sqrt{9} = \sqrt{3^2} = 3\)
\(\sqrt{16} = \sqrt{4^2} = 4\)
\(\sqrt{25} = \sqrt{5^2} = 5\)

However, Square roots can also be irrational numbers, meaning their decimal representations go on forever without repeating.
For instance, \(\sqrt{2}\) (the square root of 2) is approximately 1.41421356...

    1.3 Square Root Properties
    Product Property:
    The square root of a product is equal to the product of the square roots of the factors.
    \(\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} × \sqrt{b}\)
    Quotient Property:
    The square root of a quotient is equal to the quotient of the square roots of the numerator and the denominator.
    \( \frac{ \sqrt{a}}{ \sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}}\)
    Zero Square Root:
    The square root of zero is zero.
    Mathematically, \(\sqrt{0} = 0\)
    Addition and Subtraction:
    Unlike multiplication and division, square roots do not distribute over addition and subtraction.
    For example: \(\sqrt{a + b}\) is NOT necessarily \(\sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b} \)
    1.4 Solving \(x^2 = a\) with Square Roots

When you have an equation in the form \(x^2 = a\), you can solve for \(x\) by taking the square root of both sides.
This means when \(x^2 = a\), then \(\sqrt(x^2) = \sqrt{a}\)

However, we need to keep in mind that when we take the square root, we often need to consider both the positive and negative possibilities to cover all potential solutions.

What does that mean?:
When we square a number, whether it is a positive or negative, we get a positive result.
ex) \((-3)^2 = 9\) and \((3)^2 = 9\)

That being said, in the equation \(x^2 = a\), \(x\) could be either positive or negative.
So, including both positive and negative square roots covers all possible values of \(x\) that satisfy the equation.

Example: Solve the equation \(x^2 = 25\)

Solution:

Step 1) Take the Square Root on both sides:
\(\sqrt(x^2) = \sqrt{25}\)

Step 2) Simplify:
\(x = \pm 5\)

Step 3) Check Your Solution:
Substitute \(x = 5\) and \(x = - 5\) back into the original equation, \(x^2 = 25\), to make sure they satisfy it.
\(5^2 = 25\) and \((-5)^2 = 25\) so both solutions are valid.

Therefore, the answer is \(x = \pm 5\)

    1.5 Square Root Word Problems

Word problems are a common part of high school math, and they're something we can't escape!
Let's tackle some examples together to get a better handle on how to approach them.

Example 1: A rectangle has a length twice as long as it is width and an area of 242 \(m^2\).
Determine the length and width of the rectangle.

Solution:

Step 1) Understand the problem:
We're given that the rectangle's length is twice its width, and the area of the rectangle is 242 \(m^2\).
We need to find the length and width of the rectangle.

Step 2) Assign variables:
Let's denote the width of the rectangle as \(w\) meters Since the length is twice the width, we can denote the length as \(2w\) meters.

Step 3) Write down the formula for the area of a rectangle:
The area \(A\) of a rectangle is given by the formula: \(A = length × width\)

Step 4) Use the given information to set up an equation:
We know that the area of the rectangle is 242 \(m^2\).
So, we can write the equation:
\(A = 2w × w\)
\(242 = 2w^2\)

Step 5) Solve the equation:
Divide both sides of the equation by 2 to isolate \(w^2\)
\(w^2 = \frac{242}{2}\)
\(w^2 = 121\)
Take the square root of both sides to solve for \(w^2\)
\(w = \sqrt{121}\)
\(w = \sqrt{11}\)

Step 6) Find the length of the rectangle:
Since the length is twice the width, we have
\(length = 2w\)
\(length = 2 × 11\)
\(length = 22\)

Step 7) Check the solution: To verify, we can calculate the area using the found values of \(w\) and \(length\)
\(A = 22 × 11\)
\(A = 242 m^2\)

Therefore, the length of the rectangle is 22 meters and the width is 11 meters.

Example 2: A rectangle has a length 50% longer than the width, and an area of 6 \(cm^2\).
Determine the length and width of the rectangle.

Solution:

Step 1) Understand the problem:
We're given that the length of the rectangle is 50% longer than its width, and the area of the rectangle is 6 \(cm^2\). We need to find the length and width of the rectangle.

Step 2) Assign variables:
Let's denote the width of the rectangle as \(w\) centimeters Since the length is 50% longer than the width, we can denote the length as \(1.5 w\) meters.

Step 3) Write down the formula for the area of a rectangle:
The area \(A\) of a rectangle is given by the formula: \(A = length × width\)

Step 4) Use the given information to set up an equation:
We know that the area of the rectangle is 6 \(cm^2\).
So, we can write the equation:
\(A = (1.5)w × w\)
\(6 = 1.5 w^2\)

Step 5) Solve the equation:
Divide both sides of the equation by 1.5 to isolate \(w^2\)
\(w^2 = \frac{6}{1.5}\)
\(w^2 = 4\)
Take the square root of both sides to solve for \(w^2\)
\(w = \sqrt{4}\)
\(w = 2\)

Step 6) Find the length of the rectangle:
Since the length is 1.5 times the width, we have
\(length = 1.5w\)
\(length = 1.5 × 2\)
\(length = 3\)

Step 7) Check the solution: To verify, we can calculate the area using the found values of \(w\) and \(length\)
\(A = 3 × 2\)
\(A = 6 cm^2\)

Therefore, the length of the rectangle is 3 centimeters and the width is 2 centimeters.
